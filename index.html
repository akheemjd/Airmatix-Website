Accuracy

The difference RTK makes

A consumer drone knows where it is to within a few metres. That's fine for photos and useless for engineering. Our aircraft receive live RTK corrections through the whole flight, so every image carries a centimetre-level geotag — and every measurement made from those images inherits that accuracy. When a project needs proof, we lay independent ground checkpoints and report against them.

Positioning

FAQ

Common questions

We're based in the Greater Toronto Area and regularly fly across Southern Ontario — Toronto, Mississauga, Brampton, Vaughan, Markham, Oakville, Burlington, Hamilton, Oshawa and the regions around them. For projects farther out, get in touch and we'll confirm availability.

Missions are flown with live RTK corrections, which puts centimetre-level geotags on every image. Final deliverable accuracy depends on site conditions and flight parameters. Independent ground checkpoints are available when a project needs the numbers verified, and accuracy expectations are stated in every quote.

Every flight is conducted by a pilot holding a Transport Canada Advanced Operations certificate, operating under Canada's RPAS regulations (CARs Part IX). Advanced certification covers operations in controlled airspace and near people, with air traffic authorization obtained whenever a mission requires it.

Georeferenced orthomosaics (GeoTIFF), point clouds (LAS/LAZ), surface and terrain models, CAD contours and linework (DXF), 3D mesh models (OBJ), and PDF reports. If your workflow needs something else, tell us and we'll confirm it before we fly.

Most sites are captured in a single visit, often inside a morning. Processed deliverables typically follow within 2–5 business days depending on site size and outputs, and your quote includes a committed delivery date.

Pricing depends on site area, the deliverables you need, and how often we fly. No two sites are alike, so we quote each project individually. The consultation is free and the written quote is fixed before we fly.
